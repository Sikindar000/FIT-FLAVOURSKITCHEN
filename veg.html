Veg Tacos

In a saucepan, combine white vinegar, water, smashed garlic, mustard seeds, coriander seeds, red pepper flakes (if using), bay leaf, sugar, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil.Add the cauliflower florets to the boiling liquid and blanch for 2 minutes.Using a slotted spoon, transfer the cauliflower to a large bowl. Add green olives, artichoke hearts, and chopped basil to the bowl with the blanched cauliflower.Pour the hot pickling liquid over the cauliflower mixture. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight
Benefits: Veggie tacos offer various benefits, such as being rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They can support a healthy diet, contribute to weight management, and provide a source of antioxidants. Additionally, plant-based tacos are often lower in saturated fats, making them a heart-healthy option.

Veg Omlette

Heat 3-4 tablespoons of olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium heat.Add the sliced potatoes and cook until they are tender and lightly golden brown. This may take about 8-10 minutes. Make sure to stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Add the chopped onion and sliced red bell pepper to the skillet. Continue to cook until the vegetables are softened. In a large bowl, beat the eggs and season with salt and pepper.Pour the beaten eggs over the cooked potatoes, onion, and bell pepper in the skillet. Make sure the eggs evenly cover the vegetables.
Benefits :A vegetable omelet can be a nutritious option, providing a good source of vitamins, minerals, and fiber. It's low in saturated fat, high in protein, and offers a variety of nutrients from the vegetables.
